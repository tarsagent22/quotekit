## Where Your Budget Goes (Phoenix-Specific Breakdown)

Phoenix labor rates are running 12–18% above national average right now. A crew that would bill $8,000 in Midwest markets is billing $10,000–$11,000 in the Phoenix metro. That's not greed — it's the same supply constraint that's driving home prices.

Here's how a typical $35,000 mid-range Phoenix kitchen breaks down:

**Cabinetry: $10,000–$15,000.** This is almost always the biggest line item. Semi-custom from Arizona cabinet retailers runs $8,000–$14,000 installed; custom work starts at $18,000. Skip solid wood door styles for Phoenix — more on that in the climate section below.

**Countertops: $4,000–$8,000.** Quartz has taken over the Phoenix market for good reason: it doesn't fade the way granite does under intense UV. Expect $60–$100/sq ft installed for a mid-range quartz slab.

**Appliances: $4,000–$8,000** for a full package (refrigerator, range, dishwasher, microwave). This is also where APS/SRP rebates can knock $100–$200 off the total — worth reading before you finalize your appliance order.

**Labor (install + demo): $7,000–$12,000.** Demolition alone runs $1,500–$3,000 in Phoenix depending on what's being removed. Plumbing and electrical work for an island or relocated appliances adds $2,000–$5,000 on top of base labor.

**Backsplash + flooring: $2,000–$4,000.** Subway tile stays popular; large-format porcelain is gaining ground in higher-end Phoenix kitchens.

## Desert Climate and Your Material Choices

Phoenix's climate eliminates certain kitchen materials from consideration — something national pricing guides miss entirely.

**Solid wood cabinet doors warp in extreme dry heat.** Phoenix summers regularly hit 110°F+, and home interiors that cycle between 75°F AC and high heat create expansion and contraction stress on solid wood. Over 3–5 years, solid wood doors commonly bow or develop visible gaps at the frame joints.

Better choice for Phoenix: **thermofoil or PVC-wrapped cabinet doors**. They're more dimensionally stable in dry heat, easier to clean (relevant in dusty desert climates), and typically $15–$25/linear foot cheaper than solid wood. Several Phoenix cabinet makers — including those in the Scottsdale and Tempe showroom corridor — now actively steer buyers toward thermofoil for this reason.

**Quartz over granite, every time.** Granite is porous and can fade noticeably under Phoenix's UV intensity, especially near windows. Quartz is non-porous and UV-stable. It's also lower maintenance — no annual sealing required. Most Phoenix kitchen designers consider granite a premium-but-problematic choice; quartz has quietly become the default.

**Monsoon season (July–September) isn't ideal for scheduling.** Contractors get pulled into storm repair and exterior work during monsoon. Availability drops, subcontractors are harder to schedule, and humidity swings can complicate cabinet installation timing. If you're mid-project during July–August, plan around it.

> **💡 APS and SRP Appliance Rebate Tip:** Both Arizona Public Service (APS) and Salt River Project (SRP) offer rebates for Energy Star-certified kitchen appliances. Dishwashers and refrigerators with Energy Star ratings can qualify for **up to $200 back** through your utility's rebate program. Before finalizing your appliance package, check your utility's rebate portal: APS customers: [aps.com/rebates](https://www.aps.com) — SRP customers: [srpnet.com/energy-savings](https://www.srpnet.com). Rebates don't require you to change contractors — just purchase qualifying appliances and submit a claim. It's one of the lowest-effort cost reductions available on a Phoenix kitchen remodel.

## Finding Contractors in Phoenix: A Realistic Approach

Phoenix has no shortage of kitchen contractors — the metro area has thousands of licensed GCs. The challenge is vetting them in a market where demand lets less-qualified operators charge premium prices.

A few things that actually matter in Phoenix specifically:

1. **Verify ROC license** — Arizona Registrar of Contractors (ROC) licensing is state-required. Check license status at roc.az.gov before signing anything. It takes 2 minutes and weeds out unlicensed operators immediately.
2. **Ask specifically about desert climate experience** — specifically cabinetry in dry heat. If a contractor pushes solid wood in a Phoenix kitchen without acknowledging the warping risk, that's a red flag.
3. **Get 3 quotes minimum** — the range in Phoenix can be $10,000–$15,000 between the highest and lowest bids for the same scope. That spread is real and worth the time investment.
4. **Verify current project timeline** — a contractor who's 4–6 months out in peak season is probably good; one who can start next week in November might be worth asking why.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does a kitchen remodel take in Phoenix?**
Most mid-range Phoenix kitchen remodels run 6–10 weeks from demo to final walkthrough. Full gut renovations with layout changes take 12–16 weeks. Note that permit processing through Phoenix Building Services can add 2–4 weeks to your timeline depending on project complexity — factor this in when planning around snowbird season.

**Do Phoenix kitchen remodels increase home value?**
The 2026 Remodeling Cost vs. Value report shows mid-range kitchen remodels recouping approximately 67–72% of cost nationally at resale. In Phoenix's hot market, that percentage has historically run slightly higher — particularly for homes in Scottsdale, Arcadia, and Paradise Valley where buyers expect premium kitchens. A cosmetic refresh in a starter home neighborhood recoup less.

**Is it worth upgrading appliances during a full remodel?**
Almost always yes — the marginal labor cost of swapping appliances during an active remodel is much lower than scheduling a separate install later. And in Phoenix, pairing your remodel with Energy Star appliances lets you capture APS/SRP rebates while you're already disrupted.

**How do I know if my quote is fair?**
A mid-range Phoenix kitchen quote in the $28,000–$42,000 range is reasonable for 2026. If a quote is 30%+ above that range, ask for a detailed line-item breakdown. If it's 30%+ below, understand what's being cut — often it's licensed subcontractors, materials quality, or warranty terms.
